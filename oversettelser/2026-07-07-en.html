Today's News – July 7, 2026 (English Translation)

Hello and happy Tuesday! Today we have two big stories: a historic football night that all of Norway will remember, and some less cheerful news about toll fees for everyone who drives in Vestland. Let's take a closer look at both!

News 1: Norway Beat Brazil 2–1 – England Awaits in the Quarter-finals

Something truly special happened in the 2026 World Cup on Monday evening. Norway played the round of 16 against Brazil – one of the best football teams in the world. Over 1.2 million Norwegians were watching on TV. What they saw was a Norwegian team playing with heart, courage, and great belief in themselves.

Erling Braut Haaland became the big hero. He scored both goals as Norway won 2–1 over Brazil. The first goal came early in the first half after a beautiful attack. Brazil equalized just before halftime, but Haaland was not done. In the second half, he scored the winning goal, and Norway could celebrate a sensational victory.

This is the second time in history that Norway beats Brazil in a World Cup. The last time was 28 years ago, in 1998. Back then, Norway also won 2–1. Many people still remember that match as one of Norway's greatest football moments. Now, a new generation of Norwegian players has created their own historic moment.

In Bergen, thousands of people celebrated the victory in the city center. People sang, danced, and shouted with joy. Some even rowed in boats – a Bergen tradition when something big happens! But the celebration also got wild. Several people had to go to the emergency room during the night, according to NRK Vestland. Most had drunk too much alcohol and needed help for minor injuries.

Now England awaits in the quarter-finals. It will be a tough match – England is one of the favorites in the tournament. But after the victory against Brazil, many believe that Norway can surprise again. Whatever happens, this Norwegian team has already written itself into football history.

News 2: Toll Road Fees in Vestland Have Increased by 13 Percent

It has become more expensive to drive in Vestland. New figures from the toll tag company AutoSync show that people in Vestland paid over 13 percent more in toll fees in the first half of 2026, compared to the same period last year.

On average, each driver in Vestland paid 422 kroner per month in toll fees. That is about 50 kroner more than before. For a family with two cars, this could mean over 1,200 kroner extra per year.

Why have toll fees increased so much? There are two main reasons. First, the discounts for electric vehicles have been reduced. Previously, EVs paid almost nothing in toll fees, but now EV drivers also have to pay more. Second, there is general price growth in society – everything is getting a bit more expensive, including toll fees.

Askøy is one of the municipalities in Norway where drivers pay the most in toll fees. Many people who live on Askøy commute to Bergen for work, and they have to pass through several toll stations every day. For these families, the price increase is felt especially strongly.

Toll fees are money that goes toward building and maintaining roads, bridges, and tunnels. Many people agree that we need good roads, but it still hurts when the bills get higher and higher. Politicians in Vestland will likely discuss this issue more in the time ahead.
