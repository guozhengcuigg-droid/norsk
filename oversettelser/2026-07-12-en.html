Today's News – July 12, 2026 (English Translation)

Good morning! Today we have two big stories. The first is about the serious conflict between the USA and Iran, which has escalated dramatically in the past 24 hours. The second is about Norway's dramatic World Cup exit against England in the early hours of Sunday.

News 1: USA-Iran War Escalates Sharply

In the early hours of Sunday, July 12, the United States carried out its third round of attacks against Iran this week. American forces have struck over 140 targets in Iran, according to the US Central Command (Centcom). This is the most extensive attack so far in the conflict.

The strikes hit missile and drone facilities, naval facilities, ammunition depots, communication networks, and coastal surveillance. Explosions were reported from several Iranian cities, including Bushehr, Asaluyeh, Bandar Abbas, and Kerman.

The background for the attacks is that Iran's Revolutionary Guard fired warning shots at a container ship in the Strait of Hormuz during the night to Sunday. The ship sails under a Cypriot flag and was hit by the shots. One civilian crew member is missing, and a fire has broken out on board. The ship has sustained significant damage to its engine room and cannot continue its voyage. The crew was forced to take to the lifeboats.

After the incident, Iran declared that the Strait of Hormuz is closed until further notice. The Revolutionary Guard says no ships may pass until the United States stops its interference. The Strait of Hormuz is one of the world's most important sea routes for oil transport. Around 20 percent of the world's oil passes through this strait. A closure could have major consequences for the global economy and oil prices.

Iran responded to the American attacks by targeting US military bases in Bahrain, Kuwait, and Qatar. Air raid sirens sounded in Bahrain, and residents were asked to take shelter. The United Arab Emirates activated its air defense against drone and rocket attacks.

President Donald Trump reportedly ordered the attacks while attending the NATO summit in Ankara, Turkey. NATO Secretary General Mark Rutte backed the attacks, saying they were "absolutely necessary." He stated that Iran had broken the ceasefire and that the US therefore had to respond with force.

The conflict between the USA and Iran has developed rapidly over the past week. Earlier in the week, Iran attacked three commercial ships in the Strait of Hormuz, leading to the first American counterattacks. The US has also withdrawn a sanctions waiver that allowed Iran to sell oil internationally. This means Iran can no longer sell oil openly in dollars on the world market.

The conflict also has direct implications for Norway. The Norwegian shipping company Odfjell, headquartered in Bergen, has already stated that it will not sail through the Strait of Hormuz. Several Norwegian shipping companies are now considering alternative routes, which could lead to higher transport costs and more expensive goods for Norwegian consumers.

The Strait of Hormuz is only 33 kilometers wide at its narrowest point and lies between Iran and Oman. Around 20 percent of all oil transported by ship globally passes through this strait. A closure could cause oil prices to rise sharply. For ordinary people in Norway, this could mean more expensive petrol and diesel, and higher electricity prices because gas prices are also affected.

This is the most serious conflict between the USA and Iran in many decades. The situation is developing hour by hour, and the international community is watching closely. Several countries have called on the parties to show restraint, but for now the conflict appears to be escalating further.

News 2: Norway's World Cup Adventure Over – England Wins After Extra Time

Norway's quarterfinal against England in the World Cup was a drama until the very end. The match was played in the early hours of Sunday Norwegian time at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami, and after 90 minutes the score was 1–1. The game went to extra time, where England ultimately won 2–1.

Norway got off to a dream start when Andreas Schjelderup gave Norway the lead. Norwegian fans around the world celebrated, but England equalized later in the match. Four minutes into the first period of extra time, England's biggest star, Jude Bellingham, pounced on a rebound from goalkeeper Ørjan Nyland to score what became the winning goal.

The match was marked by several controversial refereeing decisions. French referee Clément Turpin was in the spotlight several times. First, England defender Djed Spence was awarded a penalty after a duel with Oscar Bobb, but the referee reversed the decision after reviewing the VAR screen. Norway also argued that Nyland's clearance was stopped by a camera wire before England's equalizer, and that a Norwegian goal by Torbjørn Heggem was wrongly disallowed.

After the final whistle, several Norwegian players broke down in tears on the pitch. Goalkeeper Ørjan Nyland was deeply affected, and head coach Ståle Solbakken was visibly frustrated. "Most of the margins went against us, but that's part of football. We had them with us against Brazil, but not today," Solbakken said at the press conference.

Captain Martin Ødegaard was disappointed but also proud. "It feels pretty heavy right now. We conceded two simple goals, and we got no help at all from the referee. We got nothing. It's bitter, but it has been an adventure," he told TV 2.

An utterly exhausted Erling Braut Haaland was substituted before the final extra time period. "We should be proud, but at the same time learn from this. A World Cup quarterfinal is decided by small margins. Hopefully we can learn from this for next time," Haaland told VG.

England thus advances to the semifinal, where they will face the winner of Argentina versus Switzerland. Norway's World Cup is over, but the team's performance has received widespread praise. Norway defeated Brazil earlier in the tournament, among other results, and the team has shown they can compete with the world's best.

In Bergen and across Norway, thousands of people gathered to watch the match on big screens and in pubs. In Nygårdsparken in Bergen, there was a public celebration with a big screen, and the atmosphere was electric until the very end. Many had been queuing since the morning to get a good spot. When Bellingham scored the winning goal, the park fell silent. But after the match, the crowd honored the players with applause.

Norway has never advanced beyond the quarterfinal in a World Cup. This time, the team was extremely close to a semifinal spot, and the margins were razor-thin. Head coach Solbakken summed up the feeling: "Even though it's incredibly heavy right now, I think in a week everyone can agree that the summer of 2026 was pretty OK."

Summary

The United States has carried out its third and most extensive round of attacks against Iran, striking over 140 targets. Iran has closed the Strait of Hormuz after firing on a container ship. The crew was forced into lifeboats, and one crew member is missing. The conflict has consequences for international shipping and oil transport.

Norway lost 1–2 to England after extra time in the World Cup quarterfinal. Jude Bellingham scored the winning goal. Controversial refereeing decisions marked the match, but Norway can still be proud of a strong World Cup performance.
